Getting Started

Welcome to the UAA Capstone Library! This digital archive provides access to innovative capstone projects created by IT students at the University of the Assumption. Here's how to get started:

Search & Discover

Find projects by title, author, year, or keywords

View & Download

Access full documentation and research papers

Curated Collection

Browse approved capstone projects from all batches

System Requirements

  • Modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari)
  • Stable internet connection
  • PDF reader for viewing/downloading documents
  • JavaScript enabled in your browser
Pro Tip

You can browse and search projects without an account. However, creating an account allows you to track your activity and access additional features.

Account Management

Creating and managing your account allows you to access the full features of the library system.

Creating an Account

1 Navigate to Sign In

Click the "Sign In" button in the top-right corner of any page to access the login page.

2 Select Register Tab

On the login page, click the "Register" tab to switch to the registration form.

3 Fill in Your Details

Enter your full name, email address, and create a secure password. Make sure your password is at least 6 characters long.

4 Complete Registration

Click "Create Account" to complete your registration. You'll be automatically logged in.

Signing In

To sign in to your existing account:

  1. Click the "Sign In" button in the navigation bar
  2. Enter your registered email address
  3. Enter your password
  4. Click "Sign In" to access your account

Google Sign-In

You can also sign in quickly using your Google account. Simply click the "Sign in with Google" button and follow the prompts to authenticate.

Security Tip

Always log out when using a shared or public computer. Click your name in the top-right corner and select "Logout".

Browsing Projects

The library offers multiple ways to browse through the capstone projects collection.

Home Page Overview

The home page displays all approved capstone projects in a clean, card-based layout. Each project card shows:

  • Project Title: The official title of the capstone project
  • Authors: Team members who worked on the project
  • Year: The batch year when the project was completed
  • Abstract Preview: A brief description of the project

Filtering by Year

Use the year filter tabs below the search bar to view projects from a specific batch year:

1 Locate Year Tabs

Find the year filter tabs just below the hero section. You'll see "All Years" and individual year buttons.

2 Select a Year

Click on any year tab to filter the projects. The selected tab will be highlighted.

3 View Filtered Results

The project grid will update to show only projects from the selected year.

Sorting Projects

Use the sort dropdown to arrange projects in your preferred order:

  • Newest First: Most recent projects appear at the top
  • Oldest First: Oldest projects appear at the top
  • Title A-Z: Alphabetical order by project title

Search Features

The search functionality helps you quickly find specific projects or topics of interest.

Using the Search Bar

1 Enter Your Query

Type your search terms in the search bar located in the hero section. You can search by project title, author names, or keywords.

2 Execute Search

Press Enter or click the "Search" button to find matching projects.

3 Review Results

Matching projects will be displayed in the grid. If no results are found, try different keywords.

Search Tips

  • Use specific keywords related to the technology or topic you're looking for
  • Try searching for author names if you know who worked on a project
  • Search for technologies like "React", "Python", "Machine Learning", etc.
  • Combine year filter with search for more precise results
Search Tip

If you're not finding what you're looking for, try using broader terms or partial words. The search matches against titles, abstracts, and author names.

Viewing Projects

Learn how to view detailed information about capstone projects.

Opening Project Details

To view the full details of a project:

  1. Browse or search for the project you're interested in
  2. Click the "View Details" button on the project card
  3. A modal window will open displaying comprehensive information

Project Detail Information

The project detail modal includes:

  • Full Title: Complete project title
  • Abstract: Detailed description of the project
  • Authors/Members: Complete list of team members
  • Year: Batch year of completion
  • PDF Document: Link to view or download the full documentation

Viewing PDF Documents

Click the "View PDF" button to open the project documentation in a new browser tab. Most modern browsers have built-in PDF viewers that allow you to:

  • Scroll through pages
  • Zoom in and out
  • Search within the document
  • Download or print the document

Downloading Files

Download capstone project documents for offline access and reference.

How to Download

1 Open Project Details

Click "View Details" on any project card to open the detail modal.

2 Click Download

Click the green "Download" button at the bottom of the modal.

3 Save the File

The PDF file will be downloaded to your default downloads folder.

Copyright Notice

Admin Features

Administrative features are available for authorized users with admin privileges.

Accessing the Admin Dashboard

If you have admin privileges:

  1. Sign in with your admin account
  2. Click on your name in the navigation bar
  3. Select "Admin Dashboard" from the dropdown menu

Dashboard Overview

The admin dashboard provides:

  • Statistics: Total projects, approved/pending counts, user statistics
  • Recent Activity: Latest capstone submissions and updates
  • Analytics: File views, downloads, and site visits tracking

Managing Capstones

In the Capstones section, administrators can:

  • View all submitted capstone projects
  • Add new capstone projects
  • Edit existing project details
  • Approve or reject pending submissions
  • Delete projects if necessary
  • Filter and search through projects

Managing Users

In the Users section, administrators can:

  • View all registered users
  • Monitor user activity
  • Manage user roles and permissions
Admin Tip

Regularly review pending submissions and analytics to ensure the library stays up-to-date and properly curated.

Troubleshooting

Common issues and their solutions.

Login Issues

Cannot log in with correct credentials

Try clearing your browser cache and cookies, then attempt to log in again. Ensure caps lock is not enabled when entering your password.

Forgot password

Contact the system administrator to reset your password. Alternatively, try signing in with Google if you registered with a Google account.

Display Issues

Page not loading properly

Refresh the page using Ctrl + F5 (Windows) or Cmd + Shift + R (Mac) to clear cache and reload.

Projects not appearing

Check your internet connection. Try removing any active filters or search terms. If the issue persists, the server may be temporarily unavailable.

Download Issues

PDF not downloading

Check if your browser is blocking pop-ups or downloads. Ensure you have sufficient storage space on your device.

PDF cannot be opened

Ensure you have a PDF reader installed. Try downloading a free PDF reader like Adobe Acrobat Reader or use your browser's built-in viewer.

Browser Compatibility

For the best experience, use the latest version of:

  • Google Chrome (recommended)
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Safari (Mac)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I need an account to browse projects?

No, you can browse and search projects without an account. However, creating an account provides access to additional features and helps track your activity.

Q: How do I submit my own capstone project?

Currently, project submissions are handled by system administrators. Contact your department or the library administrator to have your project added to the archive.

Q: Can I cite projects from this library?

Yes, you can cite projects for academic purposes. Include the project title, authors, year, and "UA Research Archive" as the source.

Q: Why can't I find a specific project?

The project may not have been approved yet, or it might be from a batch year not yet added to the system. Try using different search terms or contact the administrator.

Q: How do I report an error or issue?

Contact the system administrator through your department or email the University of the Assumption IT department.

Q: Is my data secure?

Yes, the system uses secure authentication and follows best practices for data protection. Passwords are encrypted and never stored in plain text.

Q: Can I access the library on mobile devices?

Yes, the library is fully responsive and works on smartphones and tablets. Simply access the website through your mobile browser.
