## Introduction

Welcome to the UAA Capstone Library! This digital repository provides access to innovative capstone projects created by IT students at the University of the Assumption. This manual will guide you through all the features and functionalities of the system.

---

## Browsing Projects

The UAA Capstone Library makes it easy to explore all approved capstone projects through an intuitive interface.

### How to Browse:

- All approved capstone projects are displayed on the **Home page**
- Use the **year tabs** to filter projects by batch year
- Use the **sort dropdown** to arrange by newest, oldest, or title
- Click **"View Details"** on any project card to see more information

### What You'll See:

Each project card displays:
- Project title
- Author names
- Batch year
- Brief abstract
- Action buttons (View Details, Download)

---

## Searching

The search functionality allows you to quickly find specific projects or topics of interest.

### How to Search:

1. Type keywords in the search bar at the top of the home page
2. Search by project title, author name, or topic
3. Press **Enter** or click the **Search** button

### Search Tips:

- Use specific keywords related to the technology or topic
- Try searching for author names if you know who worked on a project
- Search for technologies like "React", "Python", "Machine Learning", etc.
- Combine year filters with search for more precise results

## Viewing & Downloading

Access and download project documentation easily through the system.

### Step-by-Step Process:

**Step 1:** Click **"View Details"** on a project card

**Step 2:** Click **"View PDF"** to read the document in your browser
- The PDF will open in a new tab
- Use your browser's built-in PDF viewer to read, zoom, and navigate
- You can search within the document using your browser's search function

**Step 3:** Click **"Download"** to save the PDF to your device
- The file will be saved to your default downloads folder
- Downloaded files can be accessed offline
- Use for reference, citation, or academic purposes

### Copyright Notice:

All capstone projects are the intellectual property of their respective authors and the University of the Assumption. Downloaded materials should be used for educational and reference purposes only.

## Creating an Account

Creating an account provides access to additional features and helps track your activity in the system.

### Registration Process:

**Step 1:** Click **"Sign In"** in the top-right corner
- Located in the navigation bar on any page
- Accessible from both desktop and mobile devices

**Step 2:** Select the **"Register"** tab
- Switch from the login form to the registration form
- The tab is clearly labeled at the top of the form

**Step 3:** Enter your name, email, and password, then click **"Create Account"**
- **Full Name:** Enter your complete name
- **Email Address:** Use a valid email address
- **Password:** Create a secure password (minimum 6 characters)
- Click the "Create Account" button to complete registration

### Alternative Sign-Up Method:

You can also sign in quickly with your **Google account**:
- Click the "Sign in with Google" button
- Select your Google account
- Authorize the application
- You'll be automatically registered and logged in

### Account Benefits:

- Track your browsing history
- Access to analytics (for admin users)
- Personalized experience
- Faster access to frequently viewed projects

---

## Signing In

Return to your account using your registered credentials.

### Login Process:

1. Go to the **Sign In page**
   - Click "Sign In" in the top-right corner
   - Or navigate directly to /login.html

2. Enter your email and password
   - Use the same credentials you registered with
   - Ensure caps lock is not enabled

3. Click **"Sign In"**
   - You'll be redirected to the home page
   - Your name will appear in the navigation bar

### Alternative Sign-In:

- Use **Google Sign-In** for quick access
- No password required if you registered with Google

### Troubleshooting Login Issues:

- **Forgot password:** Contact the system administrator
- **Account not found:** Verify your email address or create a new account
- **Login errors:** Clear browser cache and try again

## For Administrators

Administrative features are available for authorized users with admin privileges.

### Accessing the Admin Dashboard:

1. Sign in with your admin account
2. Click your name → **"Admin Dashboard"**
3. The dashboard will open with full administrative controls

### Administrative Capabilities:

**Dashboard Features:**
- View statistics (total projects, approved/pending counts, user statistics)
- Monitor recent activity and submissions
- Track analytics (file views, downloads, site visits)

**Managing Capstone Projects:**
From the dashboard, you can:
- **Add** new capstone projects
- **Edit** existing project details
- **Delete** projects if necessary
- **Approve or reject** pending submissions
- **Filter and search** through all projects
- **Upload** PDF documents via Cloudinary integration

**Managing Users:**
- View all registered users
- Monitor user activity
- Manage user roles and permissions
- Review user analytics

### Admin Best Practices:

- Regularly review pending submissions
- Ensure all project information is accurate and complete
- Monitor analytics to understand system usage
- Keep the library updated with new capstone projects
- Maintain proper categorization by year and topic

## Need Help?

### Technical Support:

If you encounter any issues while using the UAA Capstone Library system, please contact:

- **University of the Assumption IT Department**
- **Your Faculty Adviser**
- **Department of Information Technology**

### Common Issues:

**Display Problems:**
- Refresh the page (Ctrl+F5 on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac)
- Clear browser cache and cookies
- Try a different web browser

**Login Problems:**
- Verify your email and password
- Check if caps lock is enabled
- Try resetting your password through the administrator

**Download Issues:**
- Check if your browser is blocking downloads
- Ensure you have sufficient storage space
- Verify you have a PDF reader installed

**Search Not Working:**
- Check your internet connection
- Try using different keywords
- Remove filters and try again

### Browser Compatibility:

For the best experience, use the latest version of:
- Google Chrome (recommended)
- Mozilla Firefox
- Microsoft Edge
- Safari (Mac)

### System Requirements:

- Modern web browser with JavaScript enabled
- Stable internet connection
- PDF reader for viewing/downloading documents
- Minimum screen resolution: 1024x768

## Additional Information

### About the System:

The UAA Capstone Library is a digital repository specifically designed for the Bachelor of Information Technology program at the University of the Assumption. It serves as a comprehensive archive of student capstone projects, making them accessible for reference, citation, and inspiration for future students.

### Project Approval Process:

All projects undergo a review process before appearing in the public library:
1. Student teams submit their completed capstone projects
2. Faculty advisers review and validate the submissions
3. Administrators approve projects for publication
4. Approved projects become searchable in the library

### Citation Guidelines:

When citing projects from the UAA Capstone Library in academic work:
- Include project title
- List all authors
- Specify batch year
- Reference "UA Research Archive" as the source
- Include the date accessed

**Example Citation:**
[Author Names]. ([Year]). [Project Title]. UA Research Archive, University of the Assumption. Accessed [Date].

### Data Privacy:

- User information is kept confidential
- Passwords are encrypted and secure
- Authentication follows industry best practices
- Personal data is not shared with third parties

### Future Updates:

The system is continuously improved based on user feedback and technological advancements. Check back regularly for new features and enhancements.
